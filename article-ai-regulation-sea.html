AI Regulation Hits Southeast Asia: Vietnam Passes First AI Law

The Industrial Scribe | AI Trends  ·  5 min read  ·  March 2026

For two years, Southeast Asian manufacturers have been adopting AI tools with almost no regulatory oversight. That era is ending. In the first quarter of 2026 alone, three major AI regulations took effect across the Asia-Pacific region.

CountryRegulationEffective DateKey Requirement
VietnamLaw on Artificial Intelligence1 March 2026First standalone AI law in Southeast Asia
South KoreaAI Basic Act22 January 2026Risk classification and trust framework
ChinaAmended Cybersecurity Law1 January 2026AI security reviews and data localisation

Vietnam became the first Southeast Asian nation with a comprehensive standalone AI law. Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, and the Philippines are all drafting their own frameworks. The EU AI Act is already influencing how Asian manufacturers who supply European customers must operate.

If you supply multinational customers, your AI practices are about to become a compliance requirement — not just a productivity choice.

What This Means for Manufacturers

Data Handling Gets Scrutiny

Regulations increasingly require transparency about how AI systems process data. If you're uploading customer data to an AI tool, you need to know: where does that data go? Is it used for training? Can you delete it on request? These aren't theoretical questions anymore — they're compliance obligations.

Multinational Customers Will Ask

European customers already comply with the EU AI Act. Their supply chain audits will increasingly include questions about AI usage, data governance, and algorithmic transparency. If you can't answer those questions, you become a supply chain risk.

Documentation Becomes Critical

Most AI regulations require some form of documentation: what AI tools you use, what data they process, what decisions they influence, and how humans oversee those decisions. Starting documentation now — while adoption is early — is far easier than retrofitting it after you have 50 AI workflows in production.

The Compliance Advantage

Regulation isn't just a cost. It's a competitive advantage — if you move first. Manufacturers who establish AI governance early will be preferred suppliers for multinational customers who need compliant supply chains. The factory that can show documented AI policies, data handling procedures, and human oversight frameworks wins the audit. The factory that can't loses the contract.

Don't wait for the regulation to arrive. Prepare now, and turn compliance into a sales advantage. The manufacturers who treat AI governance as a differentiator — rather than a burden — will be the ones earning preferred supplier status when the audits begin.
